Blanquette de Veau

Image source: WIKIMEDIA COMMONS

Description

Blanquette de veau is a luscious French classic—tender veal slowly simmered in a silky white sauce, enriched with cream and a touch of lemon.

With its delicate flavors, melt-in-your-mouth texture, and comforting warmth, it’s the ultimate cozy dish that feels both elegant and homey.

Bon appétit!

Ingredients

  • 1 vegetable stock cube
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 small jar of crème fraîche (or heavy cream)
  • Flour (for thickening)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 kg veal for blanquette
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube
  • 1 small can of chopped mushrooms
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 25 cl white wine

Steps

  1. Brown the veal pieces in a little soft butter until lightly golden.
  2. Sprinkle with 2 tablespoons of flour and stir well to coat.
  3. Add 2 to 3 glasses of water, the bouillon cubes, and the wine. Stir and add more water if needed to cover the meat.
  4. Slice the carrots and onion, then add them to the pot along with the mushrooms.
  5. Simmer over very low heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add more water during cooking if necessary to maintain a gentle simmer.
  7. In a bowl, mix the crème fraîche, egg yolk, and lemon juice. Add this mixture to the pot at the last minute, stir well, and serve immediately.
